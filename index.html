A note on fiabotzolemoana and umu

  • fiabotz: colloquialism for the word fiapoto (Sāmoa). 'Fia' as in 'to want', and 'poto' as in intelligence or smarts. Generally used by some Pasifika peoples living in Aotearoa to describe a 'know it all'.
  • olemoana: 'o le' as in 'of the', and 'moana' as in sea or ocean.
  • umu: earth and stone oven. The process of preparing an umu and cooking food in this manner is a collective practice in Sāmoa and other islands of Oceania.
