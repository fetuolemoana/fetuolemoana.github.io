What does fiabotzolemoana and umu even mean?

fiabotz: colloquialism for the word fiapoto (Sāmoa). Fia as in to want, and poto as in intelligence / smarts. Generally used by some Pasifika peoples living in Aotearoa to describe a 'know it all' i.e. "don't be a fiabotz".

olemoana: o le as in of the, and moana as in sea or ocean.

also bot (short for "robot"): an automated program that runs over the Internet. Some bots run automatically, while others respond to specific input. There are many different types of bots such as web crawlers, chat room bots, and malicious bots... be a good bot. Wikipedia says "Typically, bots perform tasks that are both simple and structurally repetitive, at a much higher rate than would be possible for a human alone" #goals

umu: earth and stone oven. The process of preparing an umu and cooking food in this manner is a collective practice in Sāmoa and other islands of Oceania.
